Hardcores Influence on Rhythm Games
2000
The Bemani franchise of arcade rhythm games features the hardcore genre for the first time with the songs Starmine and 250BPM in Beatmania IIDX 4th style
2001
One of the first notable inclusions of a song from the hardcore genre is MAX 300 in the hit konami dance game DDRMAX. The chart was revolutionary because it was the first chart to be rated as a 10 on the classic DDR difficulty scale. The song continued to have massive impact throughout the series getting many remixes reimagining the classic song. The most recent remix: MAX 360 is one of the few songs in the game to feature a chart rated at 19.
2002
In 2002 Beatmania The Final - The last game in the iconic Beatmania franchise. releases which features 2 songs from hardcore subgenres
2010
In 2010 the hit dance game Pump it up introduced the hardcore charts Vacuum and Cleaner both boasting extremely difficult content that pushed the boundaries of speed and stamina in the game. These charts were eventually transformed into the song Vacuum Cleaner (very creative, I know) one year later. Vacuum Cleaner remains infamous to this day for being an extremely difficult chart to clear as one of the 28 charts rated at D26
2011
Music label Hardcore TANO*C began its collaboration with Konami at this time. The Song MAX 300 was also a qualifier for the first Konami Arcade Championship (also known as KAC)
2012
In 2012 the Bemani franchise came out with the new arcade rhythm game Sound Voltex Booth which primarily featured electronic music. Multiple hardcore songs were featured in this game on release.
2019
In 2019 the Rhythm Game WACCA was released by marvelous with Hardcore TANO*C’s backing. Nearly their entire staff contributed to the game in some form.
