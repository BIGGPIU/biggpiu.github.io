The History Of Electronic Hardcore
1990
In 1990 German Producer Marc Trauner was producing a song on a cheap mixer. Due to the mixer's quality the song would regularly get distorted. Typically after hearing the distortion he would just go back to make adjustments to fix it. Though upon hearing what was produced from the cheap mixers error Marc realized that it actually sounds good. This realization led to him creating the first Electronic Hardcore song: We Have Arrived. This song was received extremely well at the clubs and raves it was played at. This event led to the genre being greatly popularized.
At this point in time the BPMs of songs released would be between 140 and 160 BPM
1991
In the United States Lenny Dee released the first dedicated hardcore record label: Industrial Strength Records in 1991 which had a large influence on the American scene, making New York one of the largest centers of early American hardcore. At this same time DJs and producers Paul Elstak and Rob Fabrie popularized a speedier style with saturated bass-lines that soon became known as gabber: One of the many subgenres of Hardcore that would come to be.
1992
In 1992 Paul Elstak founded Rotterdam Records which became the first hardcore label in the Netherlands. During this time the label ID&T was also created which led to the conception of the Thunderdome music festival which served to spread hardcore music around Europe. Attracting thousands of young people every year.
During this time genres like speedcore were also starting to develop with songs like “Thousand” by Moby releasing which peaked at 1,015 BPM
1993
Around 1993 the genre became more clearly defined and was simply named “hardcore”
1995
In 1995 the first song to refer to itself as speedcore was released. In this year the first happy hardcore singles such as “I wanna be a hippy” and “Wonderful Days” entered the national charts and gave the genre media popularity.
